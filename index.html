Overall Analysis Questions

  1. If “flip horizon” is incomplete, what alternative indicators can reliably represent speculation?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Key Findings

  • Flip-horizon incompleteness shifts the analysis toward buyer identity and transaction signals as speculation proxies.
  • Corporate ownership and owner-occupancy diverge over time, consistent with housing financialization.
  • Corporate buyers show a liquidity advantage (cash), reshaping competitive market rules.
  • Corporate activity is spatially clustered, creating neighborhood-level hotspots and enclaves.
  • Investor types appear heterogeneous, implying different mechanisms across places.

Limitations & Next Steps

  • Data linkage: Missing flip-horizon limits citywide holding-period/profit estimates.
  • Entity resolution: “LLC” does not uniquely identify institutional ownership; linking LLC networks would strengthen claims.
  • Absentee ownership: Add mailing address / assessment records to test the “asset vault” hypothesis directly.
  • Displacement: Join eviction, rent, or mobility datasets to quantify who is most affected.

Data & Methods (Brief)

This report uses (1) transaction-level sales data (2000–2023) and (2) neighborhood-level ownership/occupancy indicators (2004–2024). Key transformations include missingness checks, robust statistics (medians), grouping by buyer type, temporal aggregation for seasonality, spatial mapping, and investor segmentation by type/size (where available).
